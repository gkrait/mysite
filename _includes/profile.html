My story

George Krait, a mathematician and Computer scientist (Computational Geometry) with additional interests in Machine learning. I work as a Research Engineer at the French Institute for Research in Computer Science and Automation, France.

During my Ph.D, I was a member of GAMBLE Team at INRIA. More precisely, I am was a part of the project Non-linear computational geometry. It is about a numerical algorithm for approximating (or let us say computing 1D-Mesh of) the singular 2D-projection of a generic curve living in 3D or a higher dimension. The main motivation is that when meshing a singular variety, classical numerical methods such as Marching Cubes may miss topological information such as connected components or intersection points, etc (In fact, this problem can be avoided using symbolic methods, however, they are restricted and slow in general). The numerical algorithm that we designed and implemented guarantees that the approximation preserves the same properties of the given curve. More details about our approach can be found in this paper where we present experiments (Section 6.1), some of which are not reachable by other methods. A brief documentation about our implementation can be found here.

In my master's program at TU Kaiserslautern, Germany I worked in computing a minimal standard basis of an algebraic object called Algebroid curve. I implemented the algorithm and its applications in Singular which is a computer algebra system for polynomial computations.
